Advantages of Fitness

  1. Fitness strengthens your bones, muscles, and joints and reduces the risk of osteoporosis.
  2. It lowers the risk of heart attacks and cardiovascular diseases.
  3. Helps manage your body weight effectively.
  4. Maintains healthy cholesterol levels and blood pressure.
  5. Reduces the risk of type 2 diabetes and certain types of cancers.
  6. Improves balance and reduces the risk of falls and injuries.

Biological Knowledge for Fitness

  1. Our mind depends on healthy fats, the heart relies on carbohydrates, and muscles need protein.
  2. An optimal diet ratio includes 50% carbohydrates, 20-30% fats, and 20-30% protein.
  3. Calories per gram: FAT = 9 kcal, Protein = 4 kcal, Carbohydrates = 4 kcal.
  4. Types of proteins:
    • Lean Protein: Contains all amino acids but minimal carbs or fats (e.g., egg whites).
    • Complete Protein: Contains carbs, fats, and all amino acids (e.g., dal-chawal).
  5. Consume at least 15g of fiber every day.
  6. Body fat represents stored energy, while protein is not stored in the body.
  7. Adjust your daily calorie intake based on your BMR to safely gain or lose weight.
  8. Walking for one minute burns ~6 calories; running for one minute burns ~11 calories.
  9. Example:
    Motlu weighs 100kg. His BMR calculation shows daily calories of 4500. To reduce body fat, he should consume 4000 calories/day to lose ~1 pound per week.
    Patlu weighs 50kg. His daily calories are 2000. To gain weight naturally, his diet should be 2700 calories/day to gain ~1 pound per week.
